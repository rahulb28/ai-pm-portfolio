Stage 06
Hypotheses
AI
Experiment-ready output — backlog intake format
H1 (Priority 1) — We believe aligning the landing page price with the ad price (or explaining the difference inline) will reduce early bounce rate for mobile users arriving from paid search because the current 45% price gap creates an immediate trust break for last-minute, price-anchored travelers.
Suggested test: A/B · Landing price format · Primary metric: Scroll depth past first price · Secondary: Booking CVR
H2 (Priority 2) — We believe adding availability scarcity signals ("Only 3 rooms left for tonight") to results cards for same-day queries will increase click-through to detail pages for last-minute intent segments because urgency cues accelerate decisions for time-constrained travelers.
Suggested test: A/B · Results card variant · Primary metric: Detail page CTR for <12hr lead queries
H3 (Priority 3) — We believe surfacing top 3 filters as persistent chips on the mobile results page will increase filter engagement and downstream booking rate because the current collapsed state adds unnecessary interaction cost for task-focused mobile users.
Suggested test: A/B · Mobile results layout · Primary metric: Filter engagement rate
AI Role

What AI does — and doesn't

AI does this
Persona-lens evaluation
Assesses whether key decision signals are visible and accessible for the active persona (e.g. urgency cues for last-minute shoppers).
AI does this
Trust gap detection
Flags mismatches between what the acquisition ad promised and what the landing page delivers — a common conversion killer.
AI does this
Finding clustering
Groups related observations into themes across funnel stages rather than returning a flat list of issues.
AI does this
Hypothesis generation
Outputs "We believe [change] will [outcome] for [persona] because [rationale]" — ready for experiment intake.
⚡ Rule-based (not AI) — more reliable for these
Price consistency: ad → landing Query continuity through funnel CTA above fold on mobile Funnel step reachability Element presence checks
Sample Findings

What it surfaces

High
Acquisition promise ≠ landing page
Ad emphasized "flexible options." Landing page had zero flexibility messaging. Immediate trust gap for flexibility-seeking users.
💡 Surface flexibility above the fold on landing → improve conversion for flexibility-intent segments
High
Price inconsistency creates trust risk
Price shown in ad didn't match first price on landing (fee display difference). Price-sensitive users saw a mismatch before understanding why.
💡 Align fee format between ad and landing → reduce early bounce for price-sensitive segments
Medium
Mobile filters hidden behind extra tap
Top decision filters collapsed on mobile. Added interaction cost reduced filter engagement and degraded result quality.
💡 Persistent filter chips on mobile results → increase filter engagement and downstream conversion
Medium
No urgency signals for last-minute shoppers
Same-day searchers saw the same generic layout as users planning weeks out. No availability scarcity, no time-sensitive framing.
💡 Urgency-aware ranking + inline availability signals for short-lead queries → improve conversion for last-minute segment
Low
Business traveler signals buried below fold
Cancellation policy and flexibility tier were low in the card hierarchy — requiring scroll before business-oriented users could evaluate.
💡 Elevate policy signals in results cards for business segments → improve click-through to detail pages
